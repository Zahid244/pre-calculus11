Glossary of Key Terms

This page defines important concepts from Pre-Calculus 11 related to quadratic functions and equations.

Axis of Symmetry

A vertical line that divides a parabola into two mirror images. For a quadratic in vertex form y = a(x - h)² + k, the axis of symmetry is x = h.

Discriminant

The expression b² - 4ac in the quadratic formula. It tells us how many real roots the equation has:

  • If D > 0: two real roots
  • If D = 0: one real root
  • If D < 0: no real roots

Factored Form

A quadratic written as y = a(x - r₁)(x - r₂), where r₁ and r₂ are the x-intercepts of the parabola.

Parabola

The U-shaped graph formed by a quadratic function. It can open upwards or downwards depending on the sign of a.

Quadratic Equation

An equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0 where a ≠ 0. It can be solved by factoring, completing the square, or using the quadratic formula.

Quadratic Function

A function that can be written in the form f(x) = ax² + bx + c. Its graph is a parabola.

Roots

The solutions to a quadratic equation. Also known as x-intercepts or zeros.

Standard Form

The common way to write a quadratic: y = ax² + bx + c.

Vertex

The highest or lowest point on the parabola. In vertex form y = a(x - h)² + k, the vertex is (h, k).
